Methodology · footnote

How we scored everything.

↓
Interview count = conversations, not files.

Param's 1 PDF with 8 user profiles counted as 8 interviews. Ashwin's 1 deck with 14 sessions = 9 verified for ranking. Anuj's 4 PDFs = 4. Same method, regardless of how the submission was packaged.

Quality bar (primary).

Surprising or non-obvious findings that change product thinking plus depth of conversation. We reward specificity over volume — but volume is the floor.

Lip-service flag.

Generic insights that could apply to any product — no specificity to Leap, GeeBee, Yocket, Test Prep, or the actual user being interviewed — get demoted regardless of count.

Mis-attributions, dedup, over-quota.

The "Patel" and "Rama" transcripts → Vijay Chauhan (the speaker). "Asish" file → Ashis Kumar Lenka. Duplicate hash-identical PDFs counted once. Over-quota credit honoured fully (Sehaj 10, Ashwin 9).

Methodology · June 2026

How we scored this cycle.

↓
Multi-cycle merge.

The two biweekly workbooks (May 16–30 and May 30–Jun 12) were merged into one monthly review. Quota is scored against the whole-month combined total per submitting unit.

Pair units credited as one.

Three "A & B" pairs — Param & Sehaj, Vishal & Aashutosh, Ashita & Anuj — each counted as a single unit against a combined quota. Sehaj's solo cycle-1 tab (17) plus the "Sehaj & Param" cycle-2 tab (15) rolls up to 32.

Distinct conversations, not row count.

Autofill-blank template rows were filtered out. Aniket's ISL-generator tabs showed ~39+~10 rows; after de-duplication only ~21 distinct counsellor sessions remained. Sample/master-sheet rows ignored (Kaustubh Arora row credited to no one).

Quality bar (primary).

Surprising or non-obvious findings that change product thinking — specific to the actual product and users. Volume is the floor; specificity is the ceiling.

Lip-service flag.

Generic insights applicable to any product, or volume without conversation depth (QA logs). Aniket's ISL-generator tabs hit quota many times over but were flagged on depth — no transcripts, identical persona on every row.

Spelling-variant re-maps.

Silent normalisation: "Debasish" → "Debashis"; "Ashis" → "Asish"; "Ankit S" → "Ankit Sherke"; "Sehaj Bajaj" + "Param Thakkar" → "Param & Sehaj" pair unit.

The breakthrough

"1:1 exclusivity is the core value proposition."

Surfaced from two independent mentor sources — directly contradicts the existing sales follow-up playbook.

Five insights that change the product
01
1:1 exclusivity is the product.

Anushree's student left a session early to dodge the LS rep at the end — "looked like an advertisement." Two mentors independently confirmed students share less (CGPA, finances) with a host present. "Topmate works precisely because it stays out of the way."

02
Mentor-as-referral > acquisition funnel.

Ananya was sent Ayushi's booking link directly. The platform facilitated the connection but never owned the discovery. A different distribution model than the funnel assumes.

03
Cultural affinity as a first-class match signal.

Rizana picked Reema specifically — both Malayali, Reema a Chevening winner. Match by lived identity, not destination or skill.

04
Post-admit users are a missing segment.

Avinandita had a Birmingham offer and needed accommodation, part-time work, SOP guidance. LS serves none of that today.

05
UG is Google-Sheet-first.

Archana runs 48 students grades 8–12 entirely on Sheets — ~60 tasks/year per Grade 10. Any UG dashboard that doesn't mirror this quarter-by-quarter model fails adoption.

The breakthrough

"Chat cannibalises LFF discovery."

Organic chat-first users (Chanchal, Lakshay) never discovered LFF — Chat + Explore already met their needs. LFF has a structural discoverability problem with its own most-engaged users.

Five insights that change the product
01
Chat cannibalises LFF discovery.

Multiple organic chat-first users (Chanchal, Lakshay) never discovered LFF at all because Chat + Explore already met their needs.

02
The LFF report isn't standalone value.

Ramita skipped the report entirely — the counsellor CTA sits too prominent and too early, undercutting the paid artifact it's meant to convert.

03
App-store SEO leaks IELTS intent.

Rutuja downloaded Leap meaning to get an IELTS prep app. Recurring pattern across the month, not a one-off — wrong-intent installs entering the study-abroad funnel.

04
Scholarship discoverability caps the rating.

Madiha tied her 7/10 explicitly to not being able to find scholarships in-app. The blocker is surfacing, not absence.

05
Multi-course selection is missing.

Alroy (ready-to-apply) couldn't select multiple courses at once — a small missing primitive blocking the highest-intent users.

🥈 Runner-up Founder's Office

Vishal & Aashutosh

Users already abroad (Daanish, Sakshi, Franto on UK student visas) get India-applicant assumptions, wrong visa paths, and ignored profile data. Chat Refine ignores rich profile the product already collected.
