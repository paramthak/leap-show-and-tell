The bet
Why we do this.
Rituals beat reminders.

A Slack reminder gets ignored. A fortnightly stage with your name on it doesn't. So we built the stage.

Specifics beat summaries.

"Improve onboarding" is noise. "Bhumi searched IELTS and signed up on counselling by mistake" is signal. We only celebrate signal.

Receipts beat opinions.

Verbatim transcripts. Named users. Time-stamped calls. If you didn't hear it from a real person, you didn't find it.

The breakthrough

"1:1 exclusivity is the core value proposition."

Surfaced from two independent mentor sources — directly contradicts the existing sales follow-up playbook.

Five insights that change the product
01
1:1 exclusivity is the product.

Anushree's student left a session early to dodge the LS rep at the end — "looked like an advertisement." Two mentors independently confirmed students share less (CGPA, finances) with a host present. "Topmate works precisely because it stays out of the way."

02
Mentor-as-referral > acquisition funnel.

Ananya was sent Ayushi's booking link directly. The platform facilitated the connection but never owned the discovery. A different distribution model than the funnel assumes.

03
Cultural affinity as a first-class match signal.

Rizana picked Reema specifically — both Malayali, Reema a Chevening winner. Match by lived identity, not destination or skill.

04
Post-admit users are a missing segment.

Avinandita had a Birmingham offer and needed accommodation, part-time work, SOP guidance. LS serves none of that today.

05
UG is Google-Sheet-first.

Archana runs 48 students grades 8–12 entirely on Sheets — ~60 tasks/year per Grade 10. Any UG dashboard that doesn't mirror this quarter-by-quarter model fails adoption.

Methodology · footnote

How we scored everything.

↓
Interview count = conversations, not files.

Param's 1 PDF with 8 user profiles counted as 8 interviews. Ashwin's 1 deck with 14 sessions = 9 verified for ranking. Anuj's 4 PDFs = 4. Same method, regardless of how the submission was packaged.

Quality bar (primary).

Surprising or non-obvious findings that change product thinking plus depth of conversation. We reward specificity over volume — but volume is the floor.

Lip-service flag.

Generic insights that could apply to any product — no specificity to Leap, GeeBee, Yocket, Test Prep, or the actual user being interviewed — get demoted regardless of count.

Mis-attributions, dedup, over-quota.

The "Patel" and "Rama" transcripts → Vijay Chauhan (the speaker). "Asish" file → Ashis Kumar Lenka. Duplicate hash-identical PDFs counted once. Over-quota credit honoured fully (Sehaj 10, Ashwin 9).
